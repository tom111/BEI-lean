# Answer: Case C closure via finite-free parameter subring

Deep-thinking math model answer to
`guides/work_packages/CASE_C_MATH_QUESTION.md` (2026-04-21).

Route chosen: **(d) finite-free parameter subring**. Avoids `*-depth`,
BH 1.5.8, non-homogeneous-NZD induction, and graded Noether
normalization. Directly applicable to the BEI deformation ring.

## Correction to the question

My stated Obstacle 2 was wrong. If `в„“ вҲҲ рқ’ңвӮҠ` is *homogeneous*, then
`A/(в„“)` is still connected graded, so by BH 1.5.6 every associated
prime of `A/(в„“)` is homogeneous and lies inside `рқ’ңвӮҠ/(в„“)`. In the
counter-example I gave (`A = K[x,y,z]`, `в„“ = x`), the prime `(x, y-1)`
contains `x` but is **not** associated to `A/(x)`; in fact
`Ass_A(A/(x)) = {(x)}`.

The genuine obstruction is different: **non-homogeneous primes like
`(x-1) вҠӮ K[x]` contain no nonzero homogeneous element at all**, so
homogeneous-NZD induction cannot reach them, while non-homogeneous-NZD
induction destroys the grading. Hence prime-by-prime induction is
wrong tool; we need a *global* replacement.

## The replacement: invariant `P(A)`

For `A` connected в„•-graded Noetherian over a field `K` with irrelevant
ideal `рқ”Ә = рқ’ңвӮҠ`, define:

> `P(A)` : вҲғ `Оё_1, вҖҰ, Оё_d вҲҲ рқ’ңвӮҠ` homogeneous of positive degree such that
>   (1) they form an `A`-regular sequence;
>   (2) `A/(Оё_1, вҖҰ, Оё_d)` is finite-dimensional over `K`;
>   (3) with `P := K[T_1, вҖҰ, T_d]` and `T_i вҶҰ Оё_i`, `A` is finite free
>       as a `P`-module.

Then `P(A) вҹ№ A` globally Cohen-Macaulay. The proof chain is:

```
A_рқ”Ә CM   вҹ№   P(A)   вҹ№   A globally CM.
```

No induction over primes is needed.

## Step 1 вҖ” construct homogeneous regular parameters

Let `d := dim A_рқ”Ә`. Inductively build
`Оё_1, вҖҰ, Оё_d вҲҲ рқ’ңвӮҠ` homogeneous such that their images form a regular
system of parameters in `A_рқ”Ә`.

At stage `i < d`: `A_i := A/(Оё_1, вҖҰ, Оё_i)`, `рқ”Ә_i := image of рқ”Ә`.
- `(A_i)_{рқ”Ә_i}` is CM of positive dimension `d - i`, so `рқ”Ә_i вҲү
  Ass((A_i)_{рқ”Ә_i})`.
- By **BH 1.5.6 (just proved)**, every associated prime of `A_i` is
  homogeneous, hence вҠҶ `рқ”Ә_i`. So no associated prime of `A_i` *contains*
  `рқ”Ә_i`.
- **Graded prime avoidance** (already proved) gives homogeneous
  `Оё_{i+1} вҲҲ рқ”Ә_i` avoiding all associated primes вҖ” so a global NZD on
  `A_i`.
- Locally at `рқ”Ә_i`, `Оё_{i+1}` is an NZD inside a CM local ring;
  quotienting preserves CM and drops dimension by one.

After `d` steps, `C := A/(Оё_1, вҖҰ, Оё_d)` has `C_{рқ”Ә_C}` Artinian local.

All tools for this step are already available:
- `GradedAssociatedPrime.isAssociatedPrime_isHomogeneous` (BH 1.5.6).
- `GradedPrimeAvoidance.exists_homogeneous_nonZeroDivisor` (NZD from
  avoidance).
- The local CM fact "NZD in max of CM local ring вҹ№ quotient CM local,
  dim drops by 1" вҖ” already in `toMathlib/CohenMacaulay/Basic.lean`.

## Step 2 вҖ” Artinian at irrelevant implies finite-dimensional

`C := A/(Оё)` has `C_{рқ”Ә_C}` Artinian, so
`(рқ”Ә_C C_{рқ”Ә_C})^N = 0` for some `N`. Lift back:
`(рқ”Ә_C^N)_{рқ”Ә_C} = 0`.

Pick homogeneous generators `g_1, вҖҰ, g_s` of `рқ”Ә_C^N`. For each `j`,
`g_j / 1 = 0` in `C_{рқ”Ә_C}`, so there is `s_j вҲү рқ”Ә_C` with `s_j g_j = 0`.

**Key sub-lemma** (new, small):
> Let `M` be a nonnegatively graded `K`-module and
> `s = s_0 + s_{>0}` with `s_0 вҲҲ K^Г—`. Then multiplication by `s` on `M`
> is injective.
>
> Proof: if `s В· u = 0`, look at the lowest nonzero homogeneous
> component of `u`; its image under `sВ·` has lowest component
> `s_0 В· u_min вү  0`, contradiction.

Applied to `M = C` and `s = s_j`: `s_j g_j = 0` forces `g_j = 0`.

Hence `рқ”Ә_C^N = 0`. Since `рқ”Ә_C` is finitely generated by homogeneous
positive-degree elements, nilpotence bounds surviving monomials, so
`dim_K C < вҲһ`.

Suggested name:
`finiteDimensional_of_connectedGraded_irrelevant_nilpotent` (or fold into
the Artinian-at-irrelevant wrapper).

## Step 3 вҖ” finite-free over the polynomial parameter subring

Statement (new, standard graded algebra result):

> Let `A` be connected Noetherian graded `K`-algebra. Let
> `Оё_1, вҖҰ, Оё_d вҲҲ рқ’ңвӮҠ` be homogeneous of positive degree. If
> `Оё` is a regular sequence on `A` and `A/(Оё)` is finite-dimensional
> over `K`, then `A` is finite free as a `K[T_1, вҖҰ, T_d]`-module under
> `T_i вҶҰ Оё_i`.

Proof sketch:
- Pick homogeneous `K`-basis `bМ„_1, вҖҰ, bМ„_r` of `A/(Оё)`, lift to
  homogeneous `b_1, вҖҰ, b_r вҲҲ A`.
- Define `ОҰ : P^{вҠ•r} вҶ’ A`, `(f_1, вҖҰ, f_r) вҶҰ вҲ‘ f_i(Оё_1, вҖҰ, Оё_d) b_i`.
- **Surjective** by degree induction on homogeneous `a вҲҲ A`: reduce mod
  `(Оё)` to get `a = вҲ‘ c_i b_i + вҲ‘ Оё_j a_j` with `deg a_j < deg a`; recurse.
- **Injective** by induction on `d`: reduce relation mod `Оё_1`; by
  inductive hypothesis over `K[T_2, вҖҰ, T_d]`, coefficients are
  divisible by `T_1`; divide by `Оё_1` (NZD) and iterate; polynomial
  coefficients can't be infinitely divisible by `T_1` unless zero.

If the `Оё_i` have different degrees, use weighted-degree induction, or
(easier) replace each `Оё_i` with an equal power `Оё_i^{N_i}`; powers of
a regular sequence remain regular.

Suggested name:
`finiteFree_over_mvPolynomial_of_homogeneous_regular_sop`.

## Step 4 вҖ” finite-free over polynomial implies global CM

Statement:
> If `P = K[T_1, вҖҰ, T_d]` and `A` is a finite free `P`-algebra, then
> `A` is globally Cohen-Macaulay.

Proof at a prime `рқ”ӯ вҠӮ A`:
- Set `рқ”® := рқ”ӯ вҲ© P`, `R := P_рқ”®`, `B := A_рқ”ӯ`.
- `B` is a localization of a finite free `R`-module вҹ№ finite flat over
  `R`.
- `R = P_рқ”®` is CM local by polynomial CM + CM-localizes (both available).
- Pick regular sop `u_1, вҖҰ, u_e вҲҲ рқ”®R`. Since `B` is flat over `R`, the
  images of `u_i` are `B`-regular. And `B/(u_i)B` is finite over the
  Artinian `R/(u_i) = (R/(u_i))_рқ”®` (already Artinian), hence Artinian.
- So `u_1, вҖҰ, u_e` is a regular sop of `B`, giving `B` CM local.

Suggested name: `isCohenMacaulayRing_of_finiteFree_over_mvPolynomial`.

Ingredients:

## Why not the other candidates

**(a) better induction invariant** вҖ” `(x-1) вҠӮ K[x]` has no nonzero
homogeneous element, so homogeneous-NZD induction can't reach it.
Non-homogeneous-NZD induction destroys grading. "Unique associated
prime вҠҶ рқ”Ә" is not preserved (example: `B = K[x,y]`, `в„“ = xy`, then
`B/(в„“)` has two associated primes `(x)` and `(y)`). Prime-by-prime
induction is the wrong frame entirely.

**(b) `рқ”ӯ*`-identity / dehomogenization** вҖ” viable for standard graded
rings via `(A_z)_0 вү… B`, `A_z вү… B[T, T^{-1}]`, Nagata-ring CM
equivalence `B_рқ”®(T) CM вҶ” B_рқ”® CM`. Mathematically clean, but requires
formalizing dehomogenization + Nagata rings вҖ” likely more work than
the finite-free route.

**(c) full *-depth (Route A)** вҖ” not necessary. The finite-free route
proves a Noether-normalization-like fact internally without needing
the full `*-depth` machinery.

## BEI-specific shortcut

If `A = S[t]/ДЁ` already has an explicit homogeneous regular sop
`Оҳ_1, вҖҰ, Оҳ_d` at the augmentation ideal (from `fullRegSeq` in the HH
infrastructure), skip Step 1 entirely: go directly to Steps 2-4 with
these explicit parameters. This gives:

1. `Оҳ_1, вҖҰ, Оҳ_d` globally regular вҖ” from BH 1.5.6 on each
   homogeneous quotient.
2. `A/(Оҳ)` finite-dimensional вҖ” often easy from the explicit form.
3. `A` finite free over `K[T_1, вҖҰ, T_d]`.
4. `A` globally CM.

The key point: flatness over `K[t]` alone is *not* sufficient to bypass
Case C (gives `t`-regularity but not CM at arbitrary non-homogeneous
primes). Once `A` is globally CM, since `t - 1` is NZD,
`A/(t-1) вү… S/J_G` is CM by the ordinary regular-quotient theorem вҖ”
**no Conca-Varbaro-style initial-ideal CM transfer needed**.
