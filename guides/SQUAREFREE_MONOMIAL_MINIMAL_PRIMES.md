# Guide: Squarefree Monomial Ideals, Minimal Primes, and Vertex Covers

## Original motivation

This guide packages the next natural `toMathlib` / CM-support target after the current
monomial-ideal prime and partial primary theory:

> minimal primes of squarefree monomial ideals = minimal vertex covers

This is the classical combinatorial description needed for the monomial-edge-ideal side
of Proposition 1.6.

## Mathematical target

For a finite simple graph `G`, the squarefree monomial edge ideal

```text
I(G) = ⟨x_u x_v : {u,v} ∈ E(G)⟩
```

has minimal primes exactly the ideals generated by variables coming from minimal
vertex covers of `G`.

This is the natural bridge from monomial ideals to graph combinatorics for the CM branch.

## Recommended Lean packaging

Do not jump straight to full Stanley–Reisner generality.
Start with the graph edge-ideal case that the BEI project actually needs.

Suggested stages:

1. define the variable ideal attached to a vertex set;
2. define what it means for a vertex set to cover all generators of the monomial edge ideal;
3. prove:
   - vertex cover -> corresponding variable ideal contains the monomial edge ideal;
   - minimal vertex cover -> corresponding variable ideal is a minimal prime;
4. prove the converse:
   - every minimal prime of the squarefree monomial edge ideal arises this way.

## Likely statement shapes

The exact names can change, but the target should look morally like:

```lean
theorem edgeIdeal_le_span_coverVars ...

theorem minimalVertexCover_iff_minimalPrime ...
```

with the prime ideals represented as `Ideal.span (MvPolynomial.X '' s)`.

## Why this matters for BEI

This is the cleanest route from the monomial-edge-ideal side of Proposition 1.6 to the
equal-dimension / equidimensionality statements used by the local CM definition.

In other words:

1. `BEI/CohenMacaulay.lean` already reduces the closed-graph initial ideal to a
   bipartite monomial edge ideal;
2. this guide supplies the minimal-prime combinatorics for that monomial edge ideal;
3. after that, the remaining task is to show the Herzog–Hibi hypotheses force the
   relevant vertex covers to have equal size.

## Warnings

- Do not detour into full simplicial-complex or Stanley–Reisner infrastructure unless it
  becomes genuinely cheaper than the graph-specialized route.
- Do not overspecify squarefree monomial ideals in full generality before landing the
  graph edge-ideal case.
- Keep the theorem statements close to the combinatorics actually used by the BEI CM
  branch.

description for squarefree monomial edge ideals, strong enough to support the next
