## TL;DR

`BEI/PrimeIdeals.lean` lines 924–1295 (~371 LOC) is the main proof
of `ker(φ) ≤ P_S(G)` — that the kernel of the `primeComponentMap` is
contained in the prime component ideal. The proof case-splits on
edge type and component membership, almost certainly across 4 or 6
sister branches.

**Estimated reduction: ~80–120 LOC.** **Risk: medium** — standard
sister-fold opportunity following the
`groebner_implies_closed`-style 4-branch carve.

## Math content

`primeComponentMap G S : MvPolynomial (BinomialEdgeVars V) K → MvPolynomial …`
identifies the variables that get killed in each connected component
of `G \ S`, leaving a polynomial ring on representatives. The kernel
is generated by the relations `x_v − x_{compRep(v)}` plus the
binomial-edge-ideal-of-complete-graph generators on each component.
The proof shows containment of an arbitrary kernel element in
`P_S(G)` by reducing it to canonical form (using the monomial-swap
machinery from the previous section, lines 296–786) and then
recognising the canonical form as a `P_S` element.

## Risks

1. **Case structure may be more complex than 4-way.** Some BEI proofs
   have nested case splits (e.g., `groebner_implies_closed` had 4 ×
   2 = 8 leaves before the carve). Stage 0 must be honest about
   what the actual case tree looks like.
2. **Helper signatures may grow.** Each case carries its own
   witnesses (component representative, swap data, etc.); the helper
   parameter list may exceed what the body saves.
3. **Heartbeat regressions** — extract sub-helpers if needed.
