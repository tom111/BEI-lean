### Definitions and Theorem

Let $G$ be a simple undirected graph on vertex set $[n] = \{1, 2, \dots, n\}$. The **binomial edge ideal** $J_G \subset S = \mathbb{K}[x_1, \dots, x_n, y_1, \dots, y_n]$ is generated by the binomials $f_{ij} = x_i y_j - x_j y_i$ for all edges $\{i,j\} \in E(G)$ with $i < j$. Let $<$ be the monomial order such that $\operatorname{in}_<(f_{ij}) = x_i y_j$ for $i < j$ (as defined in Theorem~\ref{quadratic}).

Let $i < j$ be vertices of $G$. A **path** $\pi: i = i_0, i_1, \dots, i_r = j$ is **admissible** if:
1. It is **simple**: $i_k \neq i_\ell$ for $k \neq \ell$.
2. Every **internal vertex** satisfies $i_k < i$ or $i_k > j$ for $k = 1, \dots, r-1$.
3. It is **minimal**: For any proper subset $\{j_1, \dots, j_s\} \subsetneq \{i_1, \dots, i_{r-1}\}$, there is **no path** in $G$ from $i$ to $j$ using exactly the vertices $\{i, j_1, \dots, j_s, j\}$ (i.e., no ordering of $\{j_1, \dots, j_s\}$ forms a path from $i$ to $j$).

For an admissible path $\pi: i = i_0, i_1, \dots, i_r = j$, define the **associated monomial**
\[
u_\pi = \left( \prod_{\substack{1 \leq k \leq r-1 \\ i_k > j}} x_{i_k} \right) \left( \prod_{\substack{1 \leq \ell \leq r-1 \\ i_\ell < i}} y_{i_\ell} \right).
\]
Thus, $\operatorname{lt}(u_\pi f_{ij}) = u_\pi x_i y_j$.

**Theorem.** The set
\[
\mathcal{G} = \bigcup_{i < j} \bigl\{ u_\pi f_{ij} \bigm| \pi \text{ admissible from $i$ to $j$} \bigr\}
\]
is a **reduced Gröbner basis** of $J_G$ with respect to $<$.

### Proof

The proof proceeds in three steps:
- **First Step**: $\mathcal{G} \subset J_G$.
- **Second Step**: $\mathcal{G}$ is a Gröbner basis of $J_G$ (by Buchberger's criterion).
- **Third Step**: $\mathcal{G}$ is reduced.

#### First Step: $\mathcal{G} \subset J_G$

Fix $i < j$ and an admissible path $\pi: i = i_0, i_1, \dots, i_{r-1}, i_r = j$. Prove $u_\pi f_{ij} \in J_G$ by induction on $r \geq 1$ (the number of edges).

**Base case $r=1$**: $\pi$ is the edge $\{i,j\}$, so no internal vertices, $u_\pi = 1$, and $f_{ij} \in J_G$.

**Inductive step $r > 1$**: Let $A = \{ i_k \mid 1 \leq k \leq r, \, i_k < i \}$ and $B = \{ i_k \mid 1 \leq k \leq r, \, i_k > j \}$. By admissibility (ii), every internal vertex is in $A \cup B$. Since $r > 1$ and $\pi$ simple, $A \cup B \neq \emptyset$, so $A \neq \emptyset$ or $B \neq \emptyset$.

**Case $A \neq \emptyset$**: Let $i_{k_0} = \max A$ ($i_{k_0} < i$). Define subpaths:
\[
\pi_1: i_{k_0} = i_{k_0}, i_{k_0-1}, \dots, i_1, i_0 = i, \quad \pi_2: i_{k_0} = i_{k_0}, i_{k_0+1}, \dots, i_{r-1}, i_r = j.
\]
Verify $\pi_1, \pi_2$ admissible ($i_{k_0} < i < j$).

- **For $\pi_1$** (length $k_0 \geq 1$): Simple (subpath of $\pi$). Internals $\{i_1, \dots, i_{k_0-1}\} \subset A \setminus \{i_{k_0}\}$, so all $< i_{k_0}$ (since $i_{k_0} = \max A$). Thus (ii) holds. For (iii), any proper subcollection of internals yields no path from $i_{k_0}$ to $i$ (else contradicts minimality of $\pi$).
- **For $\pi_2$** (length $r - k_0 \geq 1$): Simple. Internals $\{i_{k_0+1}, \dots, i_{r-1}\} \subset \{i_1, \dots, i_{r-1}\} \setminus A$ (no later vertices $< i$), so all $> j$. Thus (ii) holds ($> j$). Minimality as above.

By IH, $u_{\pi_1} f_{i_{k_0} i}, u_{\pi_2} f_{i_{k_0} j} \in J_G$. Now compute the $S$-polynomial:
\[
\operatorname{lt}(u_{\pi_1} f_{i_{k_0} i}) = u_{\pi_1} x_{i_{k_0}} y_i, \quad \operatorname{lt}(u_{\pi_2} f_{i_{k_0} j}) = u_{\pi_2} x_{i_{k_0}} y_j.
\]
The internals of $\pi_1, \pi_2$ partition those of $\pi$ (disjoint), so $u_\pi = u_{\pi_1} u_{\pi_2}$. Let $L = \operatorname{lcm}(u_{\pi_1} x_{i_{k_0}} y_i, u_{\pi_2} x_{i_{k_0}} y_j) = u_{\pi_1} u_{\pi_2} x_{i_{k_0}} y_i y_j$. Then
\[
S(u_{\pi_1} f_{i_{k_0} i}, u_{\pi_2} f_{i_{k_0} j}) = \frac{L}{u_{\pi_1} x_{i_{k_0}} y_i} \cdot u_{\pi_1} f_{i_{k_0} i} - \frac{L}{u_{\pi_2} x_{i_{k_0}} y_j} \cdot u_{\pi_2} f_{i_{k_0} j}.
\]
The leading terms cancel, and the tail computation (analogous to Second Step) yields $u_\pi (x_i y_j - x_j y_i) = u_\pi f_{ij}$. Thus $u_\pi f_{ij} \in J_G = (u_{\pi_1} f_{i_{k_0} i}, u_{\pi_2} f_{i_{k_0} j})$.

**Case $B \neq \emptyset$**: Symmetric. Let $i_{\ell_0} = \min B$ ($i_{\ell_0} > j$). Define
\[
\pi_3: i = i_0, \dots, i_{\ell_0}, \quad \pi_4: j = i_r, \dots, i_{\ell_0}
\]
(reversed for $\pi_4: i_{\ell_0} \to j$, but $f_{i_{\ell_0} j} = -f_{j i_{\ell_0}}$ and $\operatorname{lt}$ swaps appropriately). Internals of $\pi_3$ all $< i$; internals of $\pi_4$ all $> j$. Admissibility and $S$-polynomial analogous ($u_\pi = u_{\pi_3} u_{\pi_4}$).

#### Second Step: $\mathcal{G}$ is a Gröbner basis (Buchberger's criterion)

For distinct $g = u_\pi f_{ij}, h = u_\sigma f_{k\ell} \in \mathcal{G}$ ($i < j$, $k < \ell$), show $S(g, h)$ reduces to $0$ w.r.t. $\mathcal{G}$. Let $\pi: i = i_0, \dots, i_r = j$; $\sigma: k = k_0', \dots, k_s' = \ell$.

**Case $\{i,j\} = \{k,\ell\}$**: $S(g,h) = 0$.

**Case $\{i,j\} \cap \{k,\ell\} = \emptyset$, or $i = \ell$, or $k = j$**: $\operatorname{in}(f_{ij}), \operatorname{in}(f_{k\ell})$ ($= x_i y_j, x_k y_\ell$) involve disjoint variables (distinct vertices), so coprime (regular sequence). **Lemma** (general fact): If $\operatorname{in}(f), \operatorname{in}(g)$ coprime and $u,v$ monomials, then $S(uf, vg)$ reduces to $0$ w.r.t. $\{uf, vg\}$ (leading terms coprime after scaling; tails smaller). Here $u_\pi, u_\sigma$ use variables from internals (disjoint from $\{i,j,k,\ell\}$ by cases), so applies; reduces to $0$ w.r.t. $\mathcal{G} \supset \{g,h\}$.

**Case $i = k$, $j \neq \ell$** (symmetric for $j = \ell$, $i \neq k$; assume $j < \ell$ w.l.o.g.): Show $S(u_\pi f_{ij}, u_\sigma f_{i\ell})$ reduces to $0$. Paths share prefix: $\exists a,b$ with $i_a = i_b'$ and $\{i_{a+1}, \dots, i_r\} \cap \{i_{b+1}', \dots, i_s'\} = \emptyset$. Define
\[
\tau: j = i_r, i_{r-1}, \dots, i_{a+1}, i_a = i_b', i_{b+1}', \dots, i_s' = \ell \quad (j_0 = j, \dots, j_t = \ell).
\]
**Key property** (from admissible paths): Vertices $> j$ on $\tau$ appear in **strictly increasing order** $j = j_{t(0)} < j_{t(1)} < \dots < j_{t(q)} = \ell$, where $t(0) = 0 < t(1) < \dots < t(q) = t$, and $j_{t(c)}$ is the $c$-th such vertex (successive minima confirm increasing by global min property).

Each subpath $\tau_c: j_{t(c-1)} \to j_{t(c)}$ ($1 \leq c \leq q$) is admissible: subpath of $\tau \subset G$, simple; internals $< j_{t(c-1)}$ or $> j_{t(c)}$ (by construction/admissibility of $\pi,\sigma$); minimal (by (iii) of $\pi,\sigma$). Thus $u_{\tau_c} f_{j_{t(c-1)} j_{t(c)}} \in \mathcal{G}$.

Now, $\operatorname{lt}(g) = u_\pi x_i y_j$, $\operatorname{lt}(h) = u_\sigma x_i y_\ell$. Let $L = \operatorname{lcm}(\operatorname{lt}(g), \operatorname{lt}(h)) = \operatorname{lcm}(u_\pi, u_\sigma) x_i y_j y_\ell$, $w = y_i \operatorname{lcm}(u_\pi, u_\sigma)$. Then
\[
S(g,h) = -w f_{j\ell} = -w (x_j y_\ell - x_\ell y_j).
\]
Reduce $w f_{j\ell}$ w.r.t. $\mathcal{G}$ via
\[
w f_{j\ell} = \sum_{c=1}^q v_{\tau_c} u_{\tau_c} f_{j_{t(c-1)} j_{t(c)}}
\]
with
- $v_{\tau_1} = \frac{x_\ell w}{u_{\tau_1} x_{j_{t(1)}}}$,
- $v_{\tau_c} = \frac{x_j x_\ell w}{u_{\tau_c} x_{j_{t(c-1)}} x_{j_{t(c)}}} (1 < c < q)$,
- $v_{\tau_q} = \frac{x_j w}{u_{\tau_q} x_{j_{t(q-1)}}}$.

**Verification (algebraic identity)**:
\[
(\sharp) \quad w (x_j y_\ell - x_\ell y_j) = \frac{w x_\ell}{x_{j_{t(1)}}} (x_j y_{j_{t(1)}} - x_{j_{t(1)}} y_j) + \sum_{c=2}^{q-1} \frac{w x_j x_\ell}{x_{j_{t(c-1)}} x_{j_{t(c)}}} (x_{j_{t(c-1)}} y_{j_{t(c)}} - x_{j_{t(c)}} y_{j_{t(c-1)}}) + \frac{w x_j}{x_{j_{t(q-1)}}} (x_{j_{t(q-1)}} y_\ell - x_\ell y_{j_{t(q-1)}}).
\]
**lm chain** (by $<$): $w x_j y_\ell > \frac{w x_j}{x_{j_{t(q-1)}}} x_{j_{t(q-1)}} y_\ell > \frac{w x_j x_\ell}{x_{j_{t(q-2)}} x_{j_{t(q-1)}}} x_{j_{t(q-2)}} y_{j_{t(q-1)}} > \cdots > \frac{w x_\ell}{x_{j_{t(1)}}} x_j y_{j_{t(1)}}$. Tails smaller, so standard expression with remainder $0$.

**Equality $(\sharp)$**: Expand RHS:
\[
\frac{w x_\ell}{x_{m_1}} (x_j y_{m_1} - x_{m_1} y_j) = w x_\ell \Bigl( x_j \frac{y_{m_1}}{x_{m_1}} - y_j \Bigr),
\]
\[
\frac{w x_j x_\ell}{x_{m_{c-1}} x_{m_c}} (x_{m_{c-1}} y_{m_c} - x_{m_c} y_{m_{c-1}}) = w x_j x_\ell \Bigl( \frac{y_{m_c}}{x_{m_c}} - \frac{y_{m_{c-1}}}{x_{m_{c-1}}} \Bigr),
\]
\[
\frac{w x_j}{x_{m_{q-1}}} (x_{m_{q-1}} y_\ell - x_\ell y_{m_{q-1}}) = w \Bigl( x_j y_\ell - x_j x_\ell \frac{y_{m_{q-1}}}{x_{m_{q-1}}} \Bigr)
\]
($m_d = j_{t(d)}$). Sum telescopes:
\[
RHS = w \Bigl( x_j x_\ell \frac{y_{m_1}}{x_{m_1}} - x_\ell y_j + x_j x_\ell \sum_{c=2}^{q-1} \Bigl( \frac{y_{m_c}}{x_{m_c}} - \frac{y_{m_{c-1}}}{x_{m_{c-1}}} \Bigr) + x_j y_\ell - x_j x_\ell \frac{y_{m_{q-1}}}{x_{m_{q-1}}} \Bigr)
\]
\[
= w \Bigl( x_j x_\ell \frac{y_{m_1}}{x_{m_1}} - x_\ell y_j + x_j x_\ell \Bigl( \frac{y_{m_{q-1}}}{x_{m_{q-1}}} - \frac{y_{m_1}}{x_{m_1}} \Bigr) + x_j y_\ell - x_j x_\ell \frac{y_{m_{q-1}}}{x_{m_{q-1}}} \Bigr) = w (x_j y_\ell - x_\ell y_j).
\]
Each step divides by $\operatorname{lt}(u_{\tau_c} f_{j_{t(c-1)} j_{t(c)}})$, so reduces to $0$ w.r.t. $\mathcal{G}$.

#### Third Step: $\mathcal{G}$ is reduced

Let $g = u_\pi f_{ij} \neq h = u_\sigma f_{k\ell}$ ($i < j$, $k < \ell$; $\pi: i = i_0, \dots, i_r = j$; $\sigma: k = k_0', \dots, k_s' = \ell$). Suppose $\operatorname{lt}(g) = u_\pi x_i y_j \mid \operatorname{lt}(h) = u_\sigma x_k y_\ell$ (squarefree: vars$(u_\pi) \cup \{i,j\} \subseteq$ vars$(u_\sigma) \cup \{k,\ell\}$). Vertices of $\pi$ form proper subset of vertices of $\sigma$ (strict by $g \neq h$).

**Case $i = k = j = \ell$**: Internals$\{\pi\} = \{i_1, \dots, i_{r-1}\} \subsetneq$ internals$\{\sigma\}$. Then $i, i_1, \dots, i_{r-1}, j$ is path using proper subset of internals of $\sigma$, contradicting (iii) of $\sigma$.

**Case $i = k$, $j \neq \ell$**: $y_j \mid u_\sigma x_i y_\ell$. But vars$(u_\sigma) =$ $\{x_m \mid m > \ell\} \cup \{y_p \mid p < i\}$, so $y_j$ implies $j < i$, contradicting $i < j$.

**Case $\{i,j\} \cap \{k,\ell\} = \emptyset$**: $x_i \mid u_\sigma x_k y_\ell$ implies $i > \ell$ ($x_i$ only from $> \ell$); $y_j \mid u_\sigma x_k y_\ell$ implies $j < k$. Thus $i > \ell \geq k > j$, contradicting $i < j$.

No other cases (sharing one vertex covered). Thus no $\operatorname{lt}(g) \mid \operatorname{lt}(h)$ for $g \neq h$; $\mathcal{G}$ interreduced (binomial structure/order). Hence reduced.

\qed
